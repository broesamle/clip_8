clip_8 | Changing dimensions

3.1  Scale and resize

Scale objects by a certain transformation, typically defined by the arrangement of the instruction elements.

Grow or shrink objects by a certain amount. Compared to scaling, all objects will change their dimensions by the same amount, independent of their location or size.

Set the width or height to a certain size. Compared to grow/shrink, all objecs will have the same width/height, independent of their initial dimensions.

Scale the selected rectangle (blue). Operation transforms objects so as to transform the rectangular icon at `p0` into the the sibling (attached via straignt line). [exec_approx-dim 2 #00998A,#990000]

 ==>   :   

Shrink selected objects by the length of the arrow. Downward arrow at the upper edge of the selecor indicates to shrink the objects at their respective upper edge. [normal_execution 2]

 ==>   :   

Grow selected objects by the length of the arrow. Upward arrow at the upper edge of the selecor indicates to grow the objects at their respective upper edge. [normal_execution 2]

 ==>   :   

Grow selected objects by the length of the arrow. Right arrow at the right edge of the selecor indicates to grow the objects at their respective right edge. [normal_execution 2]

 ==>   :   

Set the height of the selected objects to the height of the vertical bar in the instruction elements. Since the instruction is attached to the upper edge of the selector, the upper edge of the objects will be changed, whereas their bottom edge will not move. [normal_execution 2]

 ==>   :   

Set the height of all elements in the selection set. An secondary selector picks the height from one of the existing rectangles. [normal_execution 2]
